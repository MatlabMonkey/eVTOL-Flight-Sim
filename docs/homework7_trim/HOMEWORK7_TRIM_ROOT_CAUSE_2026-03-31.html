What I Ran

I investigated the original Homework 7 trim workflow, then ran a fallback grouped-input trim search and full-plant linearization. The main scripts used were:

  • scripts/trim/homework7_trim_cases.m for the original legacy trim() workflow.
  • scripts/trim/homework7_trim_diagnostics.m for direct force/moment balance checks using the current analytical aircraft equations.
  • scripts/trim/homework7_manual_trim_search.m for the final grouped-input trim operating points.
  • scripts/control/homework7_linmod_manual_cases.m for linmod() around the final operating points.

Root Cause

The aircraft physics were not the main problem. A direct balance study showed that steady cruise operating points do exist at 70 m/s and 85 m/s with reasonable grouped controls. The failure was in the original full-plant trim() formulation. In the legacy setup, the quaternion-based attitude state was underconstrained, so the solver repeatedly drifted into non-physical attitudes and non-unit quaternions instead of staying near the intended cruise seed. That is why the legacy trim summary showed obviously impossible results such as near-vertical pitch, near-zero airspeed, and enormous control deflections.

Two additional cleanup items were important but were not the final root cause:

  • The original trim setup treated the 12-motor input vector as 12 independent trim variables. That had to be reduced to grouped front and rear collectives.
  • The original analytical trim seeding had a gravity-sign issue that could produce a bad initial lift estimate.

After those fixes, the plant still would not converge cleanly with the legacy trim() call, which is what pointed to the quaternion-state conditioning problem rather than a missing physical trim point.

Checks That Confirmed The Physics

The direct balance search on the current analytical aircraft equations found near-zero residual cruise points at 70 m/s and 85 m/s. A direct open-loop simulation of the full plant at the 70 m/s manual seed also reported the expected initial outputs:

  • eul_truth = [0, 0.0459, 0] rad, which is about 2.63 deg pitch.
  • vinf_truth = 70 m/s.
  • alpha_truth = 0.0459 rad.
  • beta_truth = 0.

That confirmed the full plant can represent the intended operating condition correctly even though the legacy trim() search does not stay there.

What Worked

  • Grouped front and rear collective trim variables.
  • Manual balance search on the current analytical aircraft equations.
  • Direct open-loop plant verification at the manual trim seed.
  • linmod() on the full plant around the manual operating points.

What Did Not Work Reliably

  • The legacy full-plant trim() setup in homework7_trim_cases.m.
  • Trying to let the quaternion-based trim search choose the attitude state freely.
  • Treating the 12 detailed RPM channels as independent trim inputs.

Homework 7 Trim and Linmod Summary

Date: March 31, 2026

What Was Causing the Original Trim Failure

The aircraft did not fail to trim because a steady flight condition did not exist. A grouped-input analytical search showed clean steady solutions at 70 m/s, 85 m/s, and 70 m/s with 5 degrees of bank. The failure came from applying trim() directly to the full quaternion-based plant. In that formulation, the solver had too much freedom in the full state vector, including the quaternion states, and it repeatedly drifted into non-physical attitudes and non-unit quaternion values. Earlier in the process, the raw 12-motor input vector was also over-parameterized for a symmetric cruise trim problem.

What Was Changed

A dedicated grouped-input trim helper model was added so that trim() solves only for the physically meaningful trim variables: angle of attack, grouped front and rear collective RPM, and the aerodynamic control inputs. The helper evaluates the same current aircraft force and moment equations used by the simulation framework, but it avoids the ill-conditioned full-state quaternion search. After the helper finds the operating point, that point is mapped back onto Brown_6DOF_Plant.slx and the actual aircraft plant is linearized with linmod().

How to Reproduce

From the repository root in MATLAB:

addpath(genpath(fullfile(pwd,'scripts')));
results = homework7_trim_cases;
linResults = homework7_linmod_trim_cases;

This sequence rebuilds the trim helper, runs the three homework trim cases using trim(), saves the trimmed operating points, and then linearizes Brown_6DOF_Plant at those same conditions using linmod().
